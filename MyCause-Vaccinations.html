Myths and facts regarding vaccinations

Some people believe that vaccines are only for kids and not adults. However, that is false and all adults can and should get vaccinated. Some believe that preventable disease outbreaks don't occur anymore. That is not true, because travelers, who have not been vaccinated, can contract and spread disease. Another myth is that vaccines are not safe. Vaccines are in fact safe, because they undergo extensive testing and monitoring before they are approved. Others think that they cannot control prevention. It is a fact that, timely vaccines protect you from infectious diseases. Those, who are pregnant, do not think that they can get vaccinations. The truth of it is that certain vaccinations, such as the flu and whooping cough shots protect your baby in the utero and after birth. Those were some myths and facts regarding vaccinations.
